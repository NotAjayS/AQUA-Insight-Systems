1. Tank Capacity

  • Sudden drops indicate leaks or unexpected usage spikes
  • Overfilling may cause spillage and damage
  • Regularly inspect tanks and pipes
  • Maintain buffer level for peak demand

2. Water Temperature (Â°C)

  • High temps promote algae/bacterial growth
  • Low temps reduce flow and treatment efficiency
  • Avoid direct sunlight exposure
  • Use insulation to stabilize temperature

3. pH Level

  • Fluctuations may indicate chemical spills or corrosion
  • Extreme pH harms pipes and health
  • Use neutralizing filters if needed
  • Regular monitoring ensures compliance

4. Total Dissolved Solids (TDS)

  • High TDS indicates salts/minerals/pollutants
  • Affects taste, safety and plumbing
  • Consider reverse osmosis if too high
  • Monitor changes to detect contamination

5. Turbidity

  • Spikes often precede microbial contamination
  • Rainfall/construction can cause increases
  • Continuous monitoring maintains filtration
  • Use settling tanks to reduce turbidity

6. Chlorine Level (%)

  • Disinfects but harmful at wrong levels
  • Degrades under sunlight/long distances
  • Maintain to prevent bacterial growth
  • Adjust based on demand fluctuations

7. Water Inflow

  • Reduced inflow may indicate supply issues
  • Spikes can stress treatment systems
  • Helps detect anomalies early
  • Keep steady for optimal efficiency

8. Water Outflow

  • Sudden increases signal leaks
  • Measures actual consumption
  • Compare with usage statistics
  • Maintain valves for accuracy

9. Total Usage Today

  • Reveals consumption patterns
  • High periods indicate irrigation/industry
  • Encourage conservation
  • Forecast demand better

10. Leak Detection

  • Caused by pipe wear or faulty joints
  • Early detection prevents water loss
  • Schedule regular maintenance
  • Combine with pressure monitoring
